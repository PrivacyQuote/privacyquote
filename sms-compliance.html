SMS Compliance

Last Updated: September 24, 2025

PrivacyQuote is committed to compliance with the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), state mini-TCPA laws, National and State Do Not Call (DNC) Registries, CTIA Messaging Principles (2023), and Twilio’s toll-free messaging requirements. Our platform is exclusively for licensed insurance agents to nurture real-time, vendor-purchased consumer leads using Ainsley, our AI-powered SMS platform, registered with Twilio for conversational messaging. This page outlines our SMS practices for agent communications and consumer lead nurturing.

1. Agent-Facing Platform

Our website (privacyquote.github.io) collects only agent data (name, email, phone, primary office state) via forms (Get Started, Contact Us). Consumers do not interact with our website; consumer data (e.g., phone number, name) is provided by integrated vendors with Prior Express Written Consent (PEWC) for SMS nurturing.

2. SMS Consent

For Agents: By submitting our forms, you provide PEWC for automated SMS/MMS from Ainsley or our support team, limited to:

  • Opt-In: Explicit consent via form checkboxes and a 6-digit SMS verification code.
  • Message Frequency: Up to 30 messages per session for onboarding and support, with a maximum of 60 messages per month for multiple sessions.
  • Permitted Hours: Based on your selected primary office state (e.g., 8 a.m.–8 p.m. for Arizona, Connecticut, Florida, Georgia, Maryland, Mississippi, New Jersey, New York, Oklahoma, Tennessee, Utah, Virginia, Wisconsin; 9 a.m.–9 p.m. weekdays for Texas; 8 a.m.–8 p.m. for Washington’s CEMA, RCW 19.190).
  • Opt-Out: Reply “STOP” to opt out immediately, with records retained for 5 years per TCPA § 64.1200.
  • Message and Data Rates: Standard rates may apply. Reply “HELP” for assistance.

For Consumer Leads: Ainsley nurtures vendor-provided leads with PEWC, adding a second opt-in via SMS (“Reply YES”). SMS practices include:

  • Opt-In: Vendor PEWC plus our mandatory second opt-in (“Reply YES”).
  • Message Frequency: Up to 30 messages per session for conversational lead nurturing, ending with a “STOP” request, Click-to-Call action, or a final message (e.g., “We’re here if you want to restart”).
  • Permitted Hours: Messages are sent only within permitted hours (e.g., 8 a.m.–8 p.m. for Arizona, Connecticut, Florida, Georgia, Maryland, Mississippi, New Jersey, New York, Oklahoma, Tennessee, Utah, Virginia, Wisconsin; 9 a.m.–9 p.m. weekdays for Texas, no texts after 12 p.m. Sundays; 8 a.m.–8 p.m. for Washington’s CEMA, RCW 19.190). Responses to consumer-initiated messages are permitted outside these hours.
  • Opt-Out: Consumers can reply “STOP” to opt out immediately, with records retained for 5 years.
  • Content: Conversational SMS to explore insurance options, including click-to-call functionality to connect consumers with agents for quotes.
  • Message and Data Rates: Standard rates may apply. Reply “HELP” for assistance.

3. Do Not Call Compliance

For Agents: You must provide consumer leads prescreened for DNC compliance by vendors. PrivacyQuote uses DataScrub’s API to scrub all leads against National and State DNC Registries, litigator lists, and reassigned numbers before Ainsley initiates SMS. Rejected leads are returned to you with a note (e.g., “Lead rejected due to DNC match. Please verify vendor compliance.”) and added to our internal DNC list. Excessive rejections (e.g., >5% of leads) may lead to review or removal from our platform.

For Consumer Leads: Your inclusion is contingent on vendor DNC compliance, verified by our DataScrub API. You can opt out by replying “STOP,” with records added to our internal DNC list and retained for 5 years per TCPA § 64.1200.

4. Compliance with Laws

PrivacyQuote ensures compliance with:

  • TCPA (47 CFR § 64.1200): Requires PEWC, opt-out mechanisms, and 5-year record retention for consumer SMS.
  • CTIA Messaging Principles (2023): Prohibits unsolicited messages and mandates clear consent and opt-out options.
  • DNC: Agents must provide DNC-compliant leads; we scrub via DataScrub’s API and manage opt-outs via support@privacyquote.com.
  • Mini-TCPA Laws: We adhere to regulations in:
    • Arizona: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Connecticut: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Florida (Fla. Stat. § 501.059): 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC and opt-out options.
    • Georgia: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Maryland: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Mississippi: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • New Jersey: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • New York: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Oklahoma: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Tennessee: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Texas (Bus. & Com. Code § 305): 9 a.m.–9 p.m. weekdays, no texts after 12 p.m. Sundays, PEWC required.
    • Utah: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Virginia: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Washington (CEMA, RCW 19.190): 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    • Wisconsin: 8 a.m.–8 p.m., PEWC required.
    All states limit calls to 3 attempts in 24 hours and require opt-out options.
  • Twilio: Our toll-free numbers are registered for conversational messaging, with documented PEWC and DataScrub API verification.

5. Agent Responsibilities

Agents must:

  • Provide real-time, consumer-initiated leads with verified PEWC and DNC compliance, maintaining documentation for audits.
  • Select their primary office state in forms to ensure compliance with mini-TCPA laws.
  • Indemnify PrivacyQuote against claims, fines (e.g., $500–$1,500 per TCPA violation), or damages from non-compliant leads or misuse of Ainsley.
  • Contact support@privacyquote.com or 866-580-7552 to manage DNC preferences.

6. Security and Data Handling

We use AES-256 encryption for data transmission and storage, with regular compliance audits. In case of a breach, we notify affected parties within 72 hours per CCPA/GDPR. We retain minimal data (phone, opt-out status) for 5 years for TCR audits.

7. Enforcement and Verification

We reserve the right to suspend messaging, terminate vendor integrations, or pursue legal remedies for non-compliance. We verify vendor PEWC via documented proofs (e.g., forms, timestamps) and scrub leads via DataScrub’s API. Excessive non-compliant leads (e.g., >5% rejected) may lead to agent review or removal. Non-compliance results in termination and agent liability for costs (e.g., $500–$1,500 per TCPA violation).
