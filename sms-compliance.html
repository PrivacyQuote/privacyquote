SMS Compliance

Last Updated: September 18, 2025

PrivacyQuote, Inc. ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to ensuring all SMS and MMS communications sent through our AI-powered lead nurturing platform comply with applicable federal and state laws, including the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), CTIA Messaging Principles, CAN-SPAM Act, and state mini-TCPAs (e.g., Florida Telephone Solicitation Act, Texas Mini-TCPA, Oklahoma Telephone Solicitation Act, Washington’s Commercial Electronic Mail Act). This SMS Compliance page outlines our policies and responsibilities for agents and users to ensure ethical and legal messaging practices.

1. Consent Requirements

All SMS/MMS messages require prior express written consent, obtained through a clear, non-pre-checked opt-in mechanism (e.g., a checkbox on our forms). Consent language is conspicuous, such as:

  • “By checking this box and providing my mobile number, I expressly consent to receive automated SMS/MMS messages from PrivacyQuote’s AI assistant, Ainsley, about lead nurturing, insurance inquiries, or platform updates. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for info.”
  • Double opt-in: Recipients must confirm consent (e.g., “Reply YES to continue”).

Agents integrating leads via API must ensure each lead has provided prior express written consent compliant with TCPA and state regulations before delivery to our platform.

2. Opt-Out Options

Users and leads can opt out immediately by replying “STOP” to any message. We maintain an internal suppression list to honor opt-out requests and prevent further messaging. Additional options include:

  • Reply “HELP” for support information.
  • Contact us at support@privacyquote.com or 866-580-7552 to opt out.

3. Message Frequency and Content

Messages are limited to insurance-related inquiries (e.g., discounts, vehicle details) and platform updates for agents. Key details:

  • Frequency: Varies but typically 10-15 messages per interaction; standard message and data rates apply.
  • Content: Complies with CTIA guidelines, prohibiting spam, high-risk content (e.g., illegal activities), or misleading information.
  • Reasonable Hours: Sent between 9 AM and 8 PM local time, except in active, consumer-initiated conversations.

4. Agent Responsibilities

Licensed insurance agents using our platform agree to:

  • Verify that all leads integrated via API have prior express written consent for SMS/MMS communications.
  • Ensure leads are not on federal or state Do Not Call (DNC) lists before integration.
  • Comply with all applicable laws, including TCPA, CTIA principles, and state mini-TCPAs (e.g., Florida FTSA, Texas Mini-TCPA).
  • Provide accurate and lawful lead data, ensuring no fraudulent or unauthorized submissions.

Agents are solely responsible for lead compliance. We are not liable for violations arising from agent-provided data.

5. Data Protection and Fraud Prevention

We use encryption, access controls, and secure servers to protect lead data and communications. We implement feedback loops and monitoring to detect and prevent fraudulent activity (e.g., unauthorized messaging). Lead data is not sold or shared beyond facilitating one-to-one agent-lead connections.

6. The Campaign Registry (TCR) Compliance

PrivacyQuote is registered with TCR under the “Customer Care” use case. We maintain detailed consent records and audit logs for TCR compliance. Agents must ensure their lead sources align with TCR requirements, including proper documentation of consent.

7. Indemnity

Agents agree to indemnify and hold PrivacyQuote, its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents harmless from any claims, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from their violation of TCPA, CTIA, state mini-TCPAs, or other laws related to lead data or messaging.

8. Dispute Resolution

Any disputes arising from SMS communications or this policy shall be resolved through binding arbitration in Fresno County, California, under the American Arbitration Association’s rules. Users waive the right to class actions or jury trials. Governing law is California.

9. Contact Us

For questions about our SMS compliance, contact us at support@privacyquote.com or 866-580-7552.
